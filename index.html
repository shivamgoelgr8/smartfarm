WHAT WE DO

Creating environments that care

CALCULATING TEMPERATURE

Our temperature sensors work hand in hand with humidity sensors and provide their data to a computer in the nearby building. These temperature sensors are important for computing the growth of crops. For example, the ideal temperature for growth of wheat is 15.5 degree Celsius, for jowar it is 27-32 degree Celsius and so on.

CALCULATING HUMIDITY

We also employ humidity sensors which calculate humidity or moisture content of the soil. This is extremely crucial for the growth of crops and we understand the need to ease the burden of farmers, blindly spitting water on their fields. We want to save water.

COLLECTING THE DATA

All the data from sensors is collected to a median point on the farm, this rod collectively sends all the data to a nearby building housing a computer. This is automated and uses machine learning. This data is analysed and further processed.

Using Sprinkler Irrigation

Analysed data is used to water the crops. Now no more pointless and blind splitting of water, hoping to reach each and every corner. The data is carefully analysed and that much water is applied as much is needed.

Automating the hazardous job of applying fertiliser

Our product ensures that farmers are not at constant risk of inhaling dangerous chemicals fertilisers are made of. Our sensors detect the amount of fertiliser needed and that can be carefully added by the farmers. Excessive fertiliser can result in acidity or alkalinity of the soil which damages it in the long run.

Lets see the proper working sensors in our field

Sensors have become a great way of improving the quality of soil. Sensors are ever-expanding along with the progress in science and technology. Sensors are relatively simple devices and extremely useful in Agricultural field

How do Sensors Work in Field?

The sensors are applied at an interval of every few ten metres. These bluetooth sensors calculate humidity and temperature of the soil. As we know different crops have different requirements in their humidity and temperature, and we can solve that. All the data from the sensors is collectively sent to this big antenna in the middle of the field, this is done to ensure that signals dont get weak. All these signals are transmitted to a computer.

This computer reads the data, and since we know the ideal condition of the crops we are growing, it tells the Sprinkler Irrigation system to pour as much water that will raise moisture as much needed or vice versa. This way a huge amount of water is saved and also prevents chances of waterlogging.
